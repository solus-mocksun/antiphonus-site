what it is

If you die with an extremely strong will to live, you don't simply end. You get pulled into a constructed world built around a 22-stage game. No one fully understands who made it or why. Everyone who arrives was mid-stride when their life cut short.

Survive all 22 stages and you go home. That's the rule. What nobody says out loud is what you come back as.

Rules

entry

Entry is not chosen. Players arrive in M00 — the starting stage — without explanation. The world does not introduce itself.

between stages

After clearing a stage, players enter the cities — rest zones where the game pauses. Time there is real time. Some people stay for years, taking escort work, protection jobs, the kind of labor that doesn't get named.

death

Death inside the game is permanent. There is no respawn. The stages are designed — by luck, by violence, or by the choices you make under pressure — to remove people.

The 22 Stages

Stages are named after the Major Arcana of the tarot, M00 through M21. The naming is thematic, not literal — each stage has its own rules and conditions for passing.

The Four Cities

Four cities sit between the stages. They function as real places — economies, factions, hierarchies. A leaderboard tracks cleared stages across all four. Nyx sits in the top 5 when the story begins, five years of work behind him.

life in the cities

People who've cleared stages but haven't entered the next one can stay indefinitely. Some make careers of it: escort work, protection contracts, information brokering. It is not safe, but it is stable in a way the stages never are.

This is where most of the support cast operates. The Moretti faction collapse — the event that destroyed Nyx's org — happened here.

Currency

time

The currency of Antiphonus is time — literally. Time remaining, time borrowed, time owed. The exact mechanics are deliberately opaque in the early stages. Anti works it out faster than most.

Lore

the returned

One person has cleared all 22 stages. He went home. Years had passed. Nobody was waiting. Everything was different. He came back — not out of defeat, but because everything he had was here: reputation, resources, the only people who knew who he was.

His presence in the story is the pivot — the moment Anti and Nyx realize that winning doesn't answer the question. It only changes what the question is.

who built it

Unknown. The world is too deliberate to be random. The 22-stage structure, the tarot naming, the currency system — these are designed. The question of who built Antiphonus, and why, is not answered directly. It may never be.
